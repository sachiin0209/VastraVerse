Regional Styles of India

Discover the rich tapestry of traditional clothing from different regions across India

Explore by Region

Each region of India has its own distinctive style of traditional clothing, reflecting local climate, cultural heritage, and historical influences

North Indian Traditional Attire

North Indian traditional clothing showcases the region's rich cultural heritage, with flowing garments designed to accommodate the varying climate from scorching summers to chilly winters.

Women's Attire

  • Anarkali Suits - Floor-length dresses with fitted bodice and flared bottom, originating from the Mughal era
  • Patiala Salwar - Pleated, loose-fitting trousers paired with short kurtis, traditional to Punjab
  • Phiran - Long woolen cloak worn in Kashmir, often with intricate embroidery

Men's Attire

  • Sherwani - Long coat-like garment with intricate embroidery, worn for formal occasions
  • Kurta Pajama - Light cotton tunic with loose trousers, a versatile everyday outfit
  • Bandhgala - Formal jacket with a mandarin collar, popular in Rajasthan and Punjab

Regional Specialties

Rajasthan

Known for colorful ghagra-cholis with mirror work and bandhani tie-dye. Men wear angarkha and dhoti with elaborate turbans.

Punjab

Famous for vibrant phulkari embroidery on suits and chunnis. Men's attire includes kurta-pajama with a bhangra-style turban.

Uttar Pradesh

Chikankari embroidery from Lucknow adorns elegant kurtas and sarees. Banarasi silk sarees are treasured heirlooms.

Himachal Pradesh

Woolen garments with geometric patterns and bright colors. The Kullu shawl is recognized worldwide.

Styling Tips

  • Pair Bengal tant sarees with oxidized silver jewelry and a bold red bindi
  • Accessorize Mekhela Chador with traditional Assamese gold jewelry
  • For men, complement dhoti-kurta with a traditional Bengali topor (headgear) for weddings

Featured Style

Baluchari Saree

The exquisite Baluchari saree from Bengal features elaborate silk weaving with intricate panels (pallu) depicting mythological stories and scenes from royal courts. Each saree is a masterpiece of artistry.

West Indian Traditional Attire

West Indian traditional clothing showcases vibrant colors, intricate mirror work, and elaborate embroidery, reflecting the region's desert landscapes and rich cultural heritage.

Women's Attire

  • Ghagra Choli - Full skirt with blouse and dupatta, adorned with mirror work
  • Panetar - Traditional white Gujarati wedding saree with red border
  • Koli Saree - Colorful checkered sarees worn by the fishing communities of Maharashtra

Men's Attire

  • Kediyu - Pleated, flared kurta from Gujarat worn with churidar
  • Dhoti-Sadra - Traditional Gujarati attire with a distinctive upper garment
  • Phento - Traditional turban from Maharashtra

Regional Specialties

Gujarat

Known for Bandhani tie-dye textiles, Patola silk, and vibrant embroidery styles like Kutch and Saurashtra.

Maharashtra

Famous for Paithani sarees with peacock motifs, Nauvari (9-yard) sarees, and cotton khadi fabrics.

Goa

Features Portuguese-influenced Kunbi sarees with distinctive red and white checkered patterns.

Rajasthan

Renowned for Bandhej, Leheriya, and Gota Patti work on vibrant fabrics reflecting desert aesthetics.

Styling Tips

  • Layer traditional ghagra choli with heavy silver jewelry and multiple bangles
  • Pair kediyu with a colorful turban and traditional mojari footwear
  • For Paithani sarees, complement with traditional nath (nose ring) and jhumkas

Featured Style

Gujarati Bandhani

Bandhani is an ancient tie-dye art from Gujarat featuring tiny dots forming beautiful patterns. The ghagra choli adorned with bandhani work is a hallmark of Gujarati traditional clothing.

Festival Connect

Navratri

Colorful chaniya choli for garba dance

Ganesh Chaturthi

Traditional Maharashtrian nauvari saree

Gudi Padwa

Paithani sarees and traditional jewelry

Northeast Indian Traditional Attire

Northeast Indian traditional clothing represents the diverse tribal cultures of the region, featuring distinctive weaving patterns, vibrant colors, and unique styles that vary between communities.

Women's Attire

  • Mekhela Chador - Two-piece traditional Assamese garment with distinctive patterns
  • Puan - Vibrant handwoven wraparound skirt from Mizoram
  • Rongai - Traditional garment from Arunachal Pradesh with geometric designs

Men's Attire

  • Jainse - Traditional Manipuri dhoti with decorative borders
  • Tawlhloh Puan - Formal attire for men in Mizoram
  • Dakhona - Traditional Bodo garment with symbolic motifs

Regional Specialties

Assam

Known for golden muga silk and intricately woven mekhela chador with traditional motifs.

Manipur

Famous for Phanek (sarong) and Innaphi (scarf) with unique border designs and patterns.

Nagaland

Each tribe has distinctive shawls with geometric patterns that represent tribal identity.

Meghalaya

Jainsem and Dhara from Khasi and Garo tribes feature vibrant colors and striped patterns.

Styling Tips

  • Accessorize mekhela chador with traditional Assamese gold jewelry and orchid flowers
  • Pair tribal shawls with contemporary outfits for a fusion look
  • For men, complement traditional garments with tribal headgear for authentic styling

Featured Style

Naga Tribal Shawls

Naga shawls are distinctive identity markers with intricate geometric patterns and vibrant colors. Each tribe has its own unique design, and these shawls often signify social status within the community.

South Indian Traditional Attire

South Indian traditional clothing is characterized by its elegant drapes, rich silks, and intricate designs. The region's attire reflects its cultural heritage and is known for its sophisticated craftsmanship.

Women's Attire

  • Kanjeevaram Saree - Luxurious silk sarees with rich gold zari borders from Tamil Nadu
  • Kasavu - Kerala's traditional cream cotton saree with gold border
  • Pavada Davani - A half-saree ensemble worn by young women

Men's Attire

  • Veshti - White or cream cotton dhoti worn with an angavastram (shoulder cloth)
  • Mundu - Traditional garment from Kerala, similar to a dhoti
  • Panche - Kannada version of the dhoti, often with color borders

Regional Specialties

Tamil Nadu

Known for the exquisite Kanjeevaram silk sarees with contrasting borders and traditional temple motifs.

Kerala

Famous for elegant Kasavu sarees and mundus with gold borders, perfect for the tropical climate.

Karnataka

Mysore silk sarees with delicate patterns and Ilkal sarees with distinct border designs are traditional treasures.

Andhra Pradesh & Telangana

Pochampally ikat and Gadwal sarees showcase intricate weaving techniques with geometric patterns.

Styling Tips

  • Accessorize Kanjeevaram sarees with temple jewelry and fresh jasmine flowers in hair
  • Pair traditional veshti with a silk shirt for formal occasions
  • For Kasavu sarees, opt for minimal gold jewelry to complement the gold border

Featured Style

Kanjeevaram Saree

The iconic Kanjeevaram silk saree is woven with pure mulberry silk and real gold zari. Known for its vibrant colors, contrasting borders, and intricate motifs inspired by temple architecture.

Festival Connect

East Indian Traditional Attire

East Indian traditional clothing is renowned for its intricate weaving techniques, vibrant textiles, and indigenous craftsmanship, reflecting the region's rich cultural heritage.

Women's Attire

  • Tant Saree - Lightweight cotton sarees from Bengal with distinctive borders
  • Sambalpuri Saree - Odisha's traditional ikat sarees with geometric patterns
  • Mekhela Chador - Two-piece traditional Assamese attire

Men's Attire

  • Dhoti-Kurta - Traditional Bengali attire often with intricate embroidery
  • Panjabi - Distinctive knee-length kurta popular in Bengal
  • Munda - Traditional lower garment from Odisha

Regional Specialties

West Bengal

Famous for Baluchari sarees depicting mythological scenes and Kantha embroidery on cotton fabrics.

Odisha

Known for Sambalpuri ikat and Bomkai sarees with distinctive temple borders and motifs.

Bihar

Madhubani art-inspired prints on cotton sarees and Bhagalpuri silk are regional treasures.

Jharkhand

Tribal textiles with nature-inspired motifs and vibrant colors reflect indigenous heritage.

Styling Tips

  • Pair Bengal tant sarees with oxidized silver jewelry and a bold red bindi
  • Accessorize Mekhela Chador with traditional Assamese gold jewelry
  • For men, complement dhoti-kurta with a traditional Bengali topor (headgear) for weddings
